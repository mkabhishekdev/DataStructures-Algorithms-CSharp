You need to have your own strategy to tackle problems in a duration of 30minutes/45minutes in an 
coding interview.

Below approach seems to give me CLARITY & feels intuitive:
-------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE: The point being irrespective of you seing the output, first you need to understand the problem, put it on the
         paper to discuss what is the actual problem firstly. Irrespective of you know how to solve or not on the first go,
         put question on the paper and brainstorm/ask questions.

1. ON THE PAPER/BOARD -> represent the problem & than in just plain english words write down what is your approach. You don't
   have to explain. You could say you are processing what needs to be done. Than talk out loud what you are thinking &
   keep up the discussion. Once you feel confident to some extent. CODE next

2. While Coding, Talk out loud each step what you are doing(this is VERY IMPORTANT during the interview)

3. Give your best possible thought, you don't have to worry about optimization at this stage. Optimization thoughts 
   should be a byproduct of your practice before and conceptual application.
   Even you implement wrong, the point should be you did what you had known from your knowledge like logic/reasoning skills,
   DSA skills, what data structure to use etc.
